General instructions

For each fold of the ten-fold cross-validation, find the effects of site and the two clinical covariates in the training set, remove them from both the training and the test sets, fit a logistic regression with first-order interactions in the training set, and predict the response in the test set.

Example for fold 1

  1. Find the effects of the site and the two clinical covariates using individuals labeled to be in folds 2 to 10 (the "training set"). To find the effects of the covariates, fit linear regressions. To find the effects of the site, fit the MAREoS of interest.
  2. Remove the effects of the covariates and the site from these individuals and fit a logistic regression with first-order interactions. To remove the effects, use the models fitted at step 1. In the logistic regression, the dependent variable is the response to the treatment, and the independent variables are the subcortical volumes and their first-order interactions.
  3. Remove the effects of the covariates and the site from individuals labeled to be in fold 1 (the "test set") and predict their response to the treatment. To remove the effects, use the models fitted at step 1.
  4. Find the sensitivity, specificity, and balanced accuracy.
